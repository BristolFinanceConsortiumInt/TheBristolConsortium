Instrument Details

Standby Letters of Credit (SBLC)

A Standby Letter of Credit is a financial guarantee issued by a bank on behalf of a client, used as a "payment of last resort" should the client fail to fulfill a contractual commitment. SBLCs are commonly used in international trade and project financing.

  • Acts as a guarantee of payment or performance
  • Used in international trade transactions
  • Issued based on client's creditworthiness
  • Can be used as collateral for loans
  • Typically valid for one year, renewable
  • Governed by ISP98 or UCP600 rules
  • Can be confirmed or unconfirmed
  • Transferable or non-transferable options

Bank Guarantees

A Bank Guarantee is a promise from a bank that ensures a debtor's liabilities will be met if the debtor fails to settle a debt. It serves as a safety net for the beneficiary in case the applicant doesn't fulfill their contractual obligations.

  • Direct and indirect guarantee options
  • Financial and performance guarantees
  • Tender guarantees for bidding processes
  • Payment guarantees for suppliers
  • Customized terms and conditions
  • International acceptance
  • Risk mitigation for business transactions
  • Flexible validity periods

Letters of Credit

A Letter of Credit is a document issued by a bank guaranteeing a buyer's payment to a seller will be received on time and for the correct amount. It is one of the most secure instruments available for international traders.

  • Sight and usance LC options
  • Revocable and irrevocable LCs
  • Confirmed and unconfirmed LCs
  • Transferable Letters of Credit
  • Back-to-Back Letters of Credit
  • Revolving Letters of Credit
  • Red Clause Letters of Credit
  • Green Clause Letters of Credit

Documentary Credits

Documentary Credits are Letters of Credit that require specific documents to be presented before payment is made. These documents typically include bills of lading, commercial invoices, insurance documents, and other shipping documents.

  • Full compliance with UCP 600 standards
  • Document verification and validation
  • Risk mitigation through proper documentation
  • Expert advice on documentary requirements
  • Efficient processing and settlement
  • Global network of correspondent banks
  • Document preparation assistance
  • Compliance checking services

International Bill of Exchange

An International Bill of Exchange is a negotiable instrument that contains an unconditional order to pay a specified amount of money to a specific person or to the bearer of the instrument, at a fixed or determinable future time.

  • Negotiable instruments for trade settlements
  • Unconditional payment orders
  • Discounting and financing options
  • International acceptance and recognition
  • Risk mitigation in cross-border transactions
  • Flexible payment terms
  • Expert guidance on bill requirements
  • Efficient processing and settlement

Asset-Based Instruments

Asset-Based Instruments are financial tools that are backed by tangible assets such as real estate, equipment, inventory, or accounts receivable. These instruments provide enhanced security and value for financing and trade purposes.

  • Instruments backed by tangible assets
  • Enhanced security and collateral value
  • Asset valuation and assessment services
  • Structured financing solutions
  • Risk mitigation through asset backing
  • Customized instrument structures
  • Expert asset management advice
  • Global acceptance and recognition
